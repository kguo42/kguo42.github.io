First Look of z>1 Bars in the Rest-frame Near-infrared with JWST Early CEERS Imaging

Yuchen Guo, Shardha Jogee, Steve Finkelstein and the CEERS Collaboration

← Back to Home Read the Paper in ADS
Status: Published in ApJL, 2023

In the previous picture of galaxy formation, galaxies at z~2 or higher were considered too young to form bars, as their dynamics were dominated by turbulent motions driven by processes such as gas accretion and intense stellar feedback. Prior to JWST, the existence and influence of bars at z>2 remained observationally unexplored due to the wavelength coverage and resolution limits of earlier instruments.

In Guo, Jogee, Finkelstein et al. (2023) (Guo et al. 2023), we leverage the power of JWST Cosmic Evolution Early Release Science Survey (CEERS) NIRCam images to present the first quantitative identification and characterization of stellar bars at z>1, based on rest-frame NIR F444W images of high resolution (~1.3 kpc at z~1-3)

As shown in the figure below, I demonstrate the first evidence that barred galaxies already existed at z>2, when the Universe was only 20% of its current age. Our finding of bars at z~1.1-2.3 demonstrates the early onset of such instabilities and supports simulations where bars form early in massive dynamically cold disks. It also suggests that if these bars at lookback times of 8-11 Gyr survive out to present epochs, bar-driven secular processes may operate over a long time and have a significant impact on some galaxies by z~0.

The discovery also inspires many interesting discussions on whether bars can grow in early disks that might be dynamically hot, as well as on the impact of the dark matter fraction, feedback model, and tidal interactions on bar formation.

Figure 1: Montage of JWST F444W images showing the rest-frame NIR morphology of the six example barred galaxies presented in this Letter. The bars were identified by applying quantitative criteria to ellipse fits. The labels in the top-left of each figure show the CANDELS ID and redshift of each galaxy. The galaxies have spectroscopic redshifts of 2.136, and 2.312 represent the first z>2 highest-redshift bars quantitatively identified and characterized. The blue circle at the bottom right of each image represents the PSF of F444W image (~0.16 arcsec).

Key Findings

  • Demonstrate JWST's unique capability for high redshfit bar studies, and present first discovery of bars at z>2.
  • Selected bars have projected semimajor axes of∼2.9–4.3 kpc,
  • and have projected maximum ellipticities of∼0.41–0.53 in the rest-frame NIR.
  • Several have potential nearby companions, indicating tidal interaction being a potential pathway.
  • If these early formed bars survive out to present epochs, bar-driven secular processes may have a more signicant impact.
