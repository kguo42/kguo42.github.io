The Abundance and Properties of Barred Galaxies out to z~4 using JWST CEERS Data

Yuchen Guo, Shardha Jogee, Eden Wise and the CEERS Collaboration

← Back to Home Read the Paper in ADS
Status: Published in ApJ, 2025

To understand the role of bars in galaxy evolution, it is crucial to investigate the emergence and prevalence of bars in disk galaxies at different cosmic times. Limited by resolution and wavelength range, studies using previous-generation instruments mostly study bar fraction out to z~1.2. With the longer wavelength and sharper resolution JWST data, we are able to extend the study of bars to significantly earlier cosmic epochs.

In Guo, Jogee, Wise et al. (2025), we present the first estimate of the observed fraction and properties of bars out to z~4 (corresponding to a lookback time of ~ 12 billion years) using JWST CEERS NIRCam images. We analyze 1770 galaxies with stellar mass greater than 10^10 M⊙ at 0.5 ≤ z ≤ 4 and identify barred galaxies from 839 moderately inclined disk galaxies via ellipse fits and visual classification of both F200W and F444W images. Our results apply mainly to bars with projected semimajor axis abar > 1.5 kpc (∼2× point-spread function in F200W images) that can be robustly traced by ellipse fits.

In Figure 1, we present the bbserved bar fraction measured across nearly two decades, including our results, first exploration of bar fraction out to z~4, when the Universe were only ~13% of it's current age.

The observed bar fraction results highlight:

  • The early emergence of barred galaxies (out to z~4, when the Universe was only 13% of its current age). This is remarkable because disk galaxies at those epochs may be significantly different from z~0 disks and less conducive to bar formation.
  • From z~4 to z~0.5, we see a rising observed bar fraction barred galaxies >1.5 kpc (could be detected by our observations). The increasing bar fraction emphasizes the increasing importance bar-driven secular evolution, especially at z~1 when bar fraction >20%.
  • At the meantime, if bar fraction is truly low at z>1, other mechanisms such as gas accretion or mergers could play the more dominant role in early galaxy evolution.     Key Findings

    • Early Emergence and Rising Observed Bar Fraction: Barred galaxies begin to emerge at least as early as z∼ 3–4. The observed bar fraction rises since z∼4, highlighting the increasing importance of bar-driven secular evolution.
    • Bar Properties: For observed bars, we observe a rise in average projected bar size, and projected bar strength, indicating weak evolution in bar properties (see Figures 7 and 9 from Guo+2025).
    • Nearby Neighbors: At z>1.5, barred galaxies are more likely to have have potential nearby neighbors than unbarred control galaxies, suggesting that high-redshift bars may often form via tidal interactions (see section 4.5 from Guo+2025).
    • Compare to Simulations: Our observed bar fraction is consistent with TNG50 prediction for large bars out to z~ 4 and with Auriga simulations out to z~1.5. TNG50 predicts a much higher bar fraction due to a large population of smaller a_bar smaller than 1.5 kpc that we can not robustly detect due to PSF.
