About Me

Greetings! I'm Kay (also Yuchen :), a PhD candidate in Astronomy at the University of Texas at Austin, supervised by Prof. Shardha Jogee. I'm interested in the evolution of galaxies over the past 12 billion years, particularly how different physical processes transform galaxies across cosmic time, and shape them into the present-day systems we observe, including our own Milky Way.

My graduate research focuses on an important class of galaxies called barred galaxies, which host stellar bars —striking, iconic, and elongated structures located in the central regions of disk galaxies. In the present-day Universe, most of the massive disk galaxies, including our own Milky Way, host bars. Using multi-wavelength HST and JWST observations and state-of-the-art simulations, I explore the role of bars in driving secular evolution.

Mentorship and outreach have always been a big part of my journey. I’ve co-advised three undergraduate students on various galaxy evolution research projects, from early stages to student-led publications. I have also served as the graduate student coordinator for the UT Austin Astronomy NSF REU program for four summers, and co-taught the week-long Python Bootcamp.
